# The Agent-State Problem: Factors, Real-World Evidence, and Why the Proof Hasn't Landed

*Working analysis, 2026-07-22. Companion to `CAPSTONE_READINESS_EVALUATION.md`. Sources: this repo's own ledger/docs/research, plus current published literature and incidents (linked in §6).*

---

## 0. The claim-splitting that unblocks everything

"The agent-state problem is still not proved" is three different claims fused together. Separating them dissolves most of the confusion:

- **C1 — the problem exists and is costly.** *Proven.* By the published literature, by production incidents industry-wide, and — importantly — by this repo's own recorded dogfood failures (§2, column "Your own evidence").
- **C2 — known mechanisms prevent each failure class.** *Proven at mechanism level*, including in your own repo: amnesia resume 0/5 → 5/5; duplicate side effect 1/1/0 across arms; corrupt admissions 12–14 → 0; 24-concurrent-writer chain fork red → green after the advisory-lock repair.
- **C3 — installing those mechanisms as a governance boundary causes higher end-task success on public benchmarks.** *Unproven* — and your own D6-B result shows why: Apple's strict score stayed 1.0 in every arm even while native and sham each left a duplicate side effect the substrate blocked. The chosen yardstick is structurally blind to the benefit.

The feeling that "nothing is proved" is C3's failure bleeding backwards onto C1 and C2, which are solid. Your testing has been aimed exclusively at C3 — the hardest claim, measured on an endpoint that cannot see the mechanism's effect.

Two cross-cutting observations. First, every factor's *mechanism* was solved decades ago in a literature that never mentions LLM agents — that disjointness is exactly what made your Arrowsmith approach legitimate (§3). Second, F3+F5 are where the money is: duplicate side effects and fabricated state are the failures that make headlines and lose funds; they are also the two where your evidence is strongest and the public oracles are weakest.

## 2. How it is showing up in real time, compressed

Industry-scale: MAST (NeurIPS 2025) annotated 1,600+ traces across 7 frameworks — 14 failure modes, with inter-agent misalignment ≈37% and verification failures a top cluster. τ-bench showed the reliability collapse that best-of-k hides: GPT-4o ~60% pass^1 falls below 25% pass^8 — the *same task, same agent*, inconsistent state handling across runs. Gartner projects >40% of agentic-AI projects canceled by 2027, with engineering (not model) problems cited. Production incident analyses in 2026 name memory/state as the dominant cause layer.

Named incidents: Replit's agent (state fabrication + authority violation + irreversibility confusion, all in one event); Anthropic's Project Vend (persistent-memory absence producing repeated business errors and identity drift). Your own repo adds four dated, hash-verified incidents (§1) — which matters for the capstones because it is *first-party* data no peer can hand-wave away.

## 3. What the Arrowsmith strategy actually produced — and where it went sideways

Swanson's ABC model generates hypotheses: literature A (agent failures) and literature C (transaction processing, provenance, concurrency control) don't cite each other; the bridge terms (staleness, idempotency, provenance, authority, replay) reveal a hidden connection. Your v01 entry landed the discovery cleanly: *statistical predictors need governed operational state before their proposals can become valid actions* — i.e., **the C-discovery is pm-substrate itself**: the transaction/provenance stack transplanted to the agent boundary. That is a real literature-based discovery, and the six factors above are its decomposition.

But LBD has a hard limit Swanson himself respected: fish oil → blood viscosity → Raynaud's was a *hypothesis* until a clinical trial confirmed it. Arrowsmith generates C; it cannot confirm C. Two things then went sideways:

1. **The ABC loop went self-referential (v62–v228).** Instead of connecting new *external* disjoint literatures, the loop began taking A = your own previous proof layer and B = "that proof object can itself be questioned," yielding C = another witness/authority/admission layer. 152 "Confirmed → next SQ" entries, an 85k-line tower with no consumer. v229's Recursion Stop Rule is the correct diagnosis in your own words: the failure was "lack of compression, not absence of mechanism." Arrowsmith needs disjoint literatures; pointed at itself, it manufactures infinite regress.
2. **Confirmation was outsourced to oracles that can't see the effect.** The C-hypothesis predicts *fewer bad state effects* (duplicates, corrupt admissions, forks). Public benchmarks score *task completion*. D6-B proved these are orthogonal: strict score 1.0 in all arms, duplicates in two of them. Aiming the confirmation apparatus at task-level lift guaranteed honest nulls regardless of whether the hypothesis is true — the external review's "machinery so honest it cannot yet say anything at all."

## 4. Why the testing doesn't make sense (four root causes, each one line)

1. **Wrong endpoint:** the benefit lives in collateral-state safety; the oracles score task completion and are structurally blind to it (D6-B, confirmed by both external reviews).
2. **Saturated metric:** the capstone A/B's headline metric couldn't discriminate (C7 = C8 = 100%); the discriminating signal (corrupt admissions 12–14 → 0) was recorded but not the declared endpoint.
3. **Solo-unsatisfiable proof standard:** preregistration signers, independent witnesses, separation of authority, and 80%-power-across-ICC-range requirements cannot be met by one person with no budget — "completed cells: 0" after 150+ checkpoints is the structural consequence, not a discipline failure.
4. **Instrument recursion:** the verification apparatus (≈82k lines, 42:1 instrument-to-treatment) regrew the exact excised-tower failure mode — the proof machinery became the project.

## 5. What legible proof looks like (for you, your peers, and both posters)

Stop defending one monolithic claim ("the substrate lifts benchmark scores") and present six small paired claims, each on a **state-effect endpoint** an audience can verify by looking at two short logs:

> *For failure factor Fᵢ: under identical inputs, the baseline arm commits the failure (visible artifact: duplicate send / forked chain / corrupt admission / lost resume), and the substrate arm blocks it (visible artifact: block event + unchanged state), at measured token overhead T.*

Four of six already exist as recorded results: F1 (0/5 → 5/5), F3 (1/1/0), F4 (24-writer red → green), F5 (12–14 → 0). F2 needs the capstone `stale` scenario re-rendered as a paired demo; F6 needs one small RACI-violation scenario (an agent attempting a write outside its accountable edge). Each demo should be: one command, fixed seed, ≤30 lines of output, one poster panel. The honest headline for peers — which is *stronger*, not weaker, than an overclaimed benchmark result:

> "The failure classes are documented in the field and reproduced first-party in our own development. Each governance mechanism demonstrably prevents its class. Whether prevention converts to higher end-task scores is open — and we showed the standard benchmarks cannot even measure the difference, which is itself a publishable finding."

That last clause (the oracle blind spot) is your most defensible novel result. For the PM Methods poster it is the monitoring/control story; for the Cost Engineering poster, F3+F5 price directly (cost of a duplicate trade; cost of rework from a corrupt admission) — prevention-vs-failure cost, straight from the cost-of-quality chapters.
